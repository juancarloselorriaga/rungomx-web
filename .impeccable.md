## Design Context

### Users (Public — Athletes)

The public website should optimize first for athletes discovering events and deciding whether to register. They are comparing options, checking logistics, validating trust, and deciding whether an event feels legitimate, clear, and worth committing to. The primary design lens is conversion through confidence.

### Users (Dashboard — Event Organizers)

Event organizers are time-pressured and stressed during live events. They're not exploring; they have a job to finish. Speed and control are everything. Emotional goals: confidence, speed, relief — not delight, not wonder. Just "this works, I got it done."

### Brand Personality

**Public:** Trustworthy, clear, grounded.

**Organizer dashboard:** Bold, modern, community-driven. Peer-to-peer tone. Energy without being loud. Designed by someone who runs.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Public:** Premium editorial utility — calm, spacious, information-first. Large-format typography, generous whitespace, restrained accents.

**Organizer dashboard:** Elevated above the dashboard — flagship feature feel. oklch-based warm orange-red primary, brand blue/green accents, gold for Pro tier only. Geist Sans — lean on weight variation and size contrast for hierarchy. Light mode primary.

**Anti-references (dashboard must NOT look like):** Generic ChatGPT/LLM chat UI, Clippy-style cheerfulness, dark AI aesthetic (cyan-on-dark, glowing borders, purple-blue gradients), corporate SaaS wizard (sterile step-through progress bars).

### Design Principles

**Public site:**
1. Clarity before persuasion: decision-making information should appear before decorative storytelling.
2. Trust through proof: dates, location, pricing, organizer credibility, policies, and status should be easy to find.
3. Calm hierarchy: one clear primary action and muted secondary paths.
4. Whitespace as structure: confidence through spacing and proportion, not extra borders and cards.
5. Progressive disclosure: scan quickly, dive deeper when needed.

**Organizer dashboard:**
1. Speed over ceremony: every interaction should feel fast. Organizer's time is the most valuable resource.
2. Confidence through clarity: proposals and states must be scannable at a glance.
3. Elevated, not alien: same design tokens as the rest of the dashboard, applied with more intention.
4. Community, not corporate: peer-to-peer tone, energy without being loud.
5. Control belongs to the organizer: AI assists, it never takes over. Apply/dismiss controls always visible.
